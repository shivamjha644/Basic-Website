Armenia Posts Largest Trade Gap in Over 3 Years

The trade deficit in Armenia widened to USD 436.7 million in March 2023, almost doubling from the USD 220.2 million gap in the same month a year earlier. It was the largest trade deficit since December 2019, as imports increased 136.9 percent year-on-year to USD 1,063.3 million, while exports went up 174.1 percent to USD 626.6 million. Considering the January-March period, the country registered a trade gap of USD 918.6 million, compared to the USD 631.1 million deficit in the corresponding period last year.
